Bacon Jalapeño Macaroni and Cheese

By Connor Nielsen

Summary

This recipe is a mexican twist on the traditional american macaroni and cheese. The Jalapeños adds a hint of spice to a normally vary savory and rich dish. Bacon adds protein to a dish that normally does not have ass as well as adding some salty and smokey flavors. This mexican twist on an american classic is a great family dinner and is a perfect side diah for any family dinner.

Recipe Information

  • Prep time: 30 min
  • Cook time: 30 min
  • Servings: 8
  • Calories: 839

Ingredients

  • 3 cups dry elbow macaroni
  • ¼ cup butter
  • 2 large fresh jalapenBacon Jalapeños, seeded, deveined and finely chopped
  • 1⁄3 cup all purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups half-and-half
  • 3½ cups shredded extra sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1½ cups shredded 4 cheese Mexican blend
  • ¼ tsp. sea salt
  • ¼ tsp. cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp. dry mustard powder
  • 1 pkg. (12 oz.) smoked bacon, cooked crisp and crumbled
  • ¾ cup plain panko crumbs
  • 2 Tbsp. melted butter

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F
  2. Bring 4 quarts of water to a boil in a large stock pot and once it is boiling add the dry macaroni and cook for 9 minutes. When the macaroni is ready drain it and set aside.
  3. Melt the butter in a large stock pot and add minced jalapeño. Cook the jalapeño for 1 minute then add flour and cook for 2-3 minutes. Whisk in the milk and half-and-half and bring to a boil. Cook for 2-3 minutes until thickened, whisking continually.
  4. Remove the stock pot from heat and add cheeses, salt, peppers and mustard. Stir until cheeses are melted then add bacon and macaroni. Stir ingredients until fully combined.
  5. Pour the contents of the pot into a 3 qt baking dish sprayed with nonstick cooking spray.
  6. Combine the panko crumbs with 2 tablespoons of melted butter and sprinkle over the top of the macaroni and cheese.
  7. Bake 30 minutes until the top is golden brown
